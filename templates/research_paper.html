Literature Review

The detection of plant diseases has benefited greatly from recent developments in AI. Diverse datasets, constrained hardware resources, or the requirement for real-time accuracy in smart agriculture settings present unique challenges that have been addressed by a variety of DL approaches and architectures.

Researchers have explored various techniques to improve plant disease detection. Maurya et al. proposed a lightweight meta-ensemble approach combining LSTM and MLP-Mixer models, ideal for deployment on MCUs with over 98% accuracy. Zhang et al. combined residual and capsule networks to create SE-SK-CapResNet, achieving 98.58% accuracy with improved robustness against image distortion.

Several studies have focused on specific crops. Khattak et al. addressed disease classification in citrus fruits with 95.65% accuracy, while Abinaya et al. developed CAAR-UNet for segmentation with 95.26% accuracy. Altabaji et al. showed Modified LeafNet outperforming other models for rice disease classification with 97.44% validation accuracy.

Recent advancements include Amin et al.'s feature-fusion model using EfficientNetB0 and DenseNet121 (98.56% accuracy), Yang et al.'s transformer-enhanced YOLOv8n model for rice pest detection (93.6% mAP), and Moupojou et al.'s FieldPlant dataset that improved detection performance over PlantDoc.

Practical applications have also emerged, such as Lin et al.'s StrawberryTalk IoT platform (97.92% accuracy) and Coletta et al.'s crowdsensing-based strategy using the Nuru mobile app for rural farming areas. These studies collectively indicate a strong shift toward lightweight, field-adaptable models that incorporate domain-specific optimizations.
